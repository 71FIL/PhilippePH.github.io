The Relevance of Web Privacy for Everyday Users

What information is collected?

Sometimes identifying information (name, email, etc.). Otherwise, a lot of trackers attempts to have a uniquely identifier for users, even if they cannot always tie to whom it is associates

How is the information collected, and who collects it?

Cookies, scripts, requests. Websites, third-party

How abundant is web tracking?

Basically, everywhere. Found on 90% of websites. Cookies most widely used. Can patch togetehr up to 90% of browsing history. Can be used by intelligence agencies as well.

Why should you care?

  • Data Ownership: Own your data, so that you can decide what to do with it. Once your data is out there, it ain't coming back.
  • Protecting yourself from data breaches (security)
  • Avoiding Manipulation: The data collected by cookies and trackers can be used to manipulate your online experience. For example, you may see biased news or content that reinforces your existing beliefs, contributing to echo chambers and polarization. Cambridge Analytica & Voting Influence.
  • Freedom of Speech and Freedom of Opinion
  • Future Impact: The data collected today can have long-term consequences. Even if you're not concerned about privacy today, you might be in the future when data about your online activities could be used against you in unforeseen ways.
